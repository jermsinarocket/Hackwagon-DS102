A Study on the Key Factors Affecting the
Purchasing Trend of HDB's Dwellings in Singapore


Executive Summary:¶

The objective of this research is to analyze and identify the key factors affecting the purchasing Trend of HDB's Dwellings in Singapore. The relevant datasets and the required features have been identified and Date Cleaning, Web Scraping and Exploratory Data Analysis Techniques have been performed on these datasets to perform a comprehensive and accurate analysis of the defined research area. Factors such as the types of dwelling units, regions that the dwelling units are in, along with the remaining lease (years) and availability of amenities, were identified to have played an important role in determining the popularity of these dwelling units.

Problem Statement:¶

Housing Development Board (HDB) flats are the most commonly lived in form of accomodation and more than 80% of Singapore's Population live in HDB flats. Public housing precincts in Singapore comprises of an average of 10 blocks of HDB flats per precinct and they are collectively grouped into up to nine neighbourhoods per new town (Building Area Cluster). These towns are subsequently grouped into the primary four regions of Singapore - North, West, North East, Central and East. It is commonly known that the popularity (number of purchases) of HDB Flats are largely affected by the location that they are in but the true factors affecting the popularity of HDB flats are less known to the general public. Exploratory Data Analysis Techniques will be applied to analyze the key factors affecting the the purchasing trend (popularity) of these HDB Flats.

Research Topic¶

(A HDB Dwelling Unit refers to a HDB Residential Unit)

The research topic that I have chosen will be the study on the key factors affecting Singapore's purchasing trend of HDB's Dwellings.The scope of my research will cover only sold HDB Residential Flats that ranges from 1-Room to Executive Flats only, up to and inclusive of the year 2019.

To identify and analyze the key factors affecting the number of dwelling units sold in Singapore, I have identified several **key focus areas** for my research and they are as follow:

  1. Dwelling Units Sold based on Building Year Completion of Residential Areas (Remaining Lease)
    • Analyzing whether the Building Year Completion (Remaining Lease) of Residential Areas affect the number of dwelling units sold by type for that area.
  1. Distribution of Dwelling Units sold
    • Analyzing how the dwelling units sold are distributed across the residential areas of Singapore.
  1. Percentage of Dwelling Units Sold By Type
    • Analyzing what is the most common type of dwelling units sold in Singapore.
  1. Dwelling Units Sold Based on Residential and Commercial Tag
    • Analyzing whether the number of dwelling units sold are affected by the presence of commercial facilities.
  1. Dwelling Units Sold (by type) Based on Amenities Availability
    • Analyzing whether the number of dwelling units sold (by type) are affected by the presence of amenities.
  1. Number of Dwelling Units Sold by Region (Using Heatmap and Bubble Map)
    • Analyzing whether the region in which the dwelling units are located in affect the number of units sold (We will be analyzing based on region as we are unable to obtain the exact longituide and latitude (Limitations of GeoCode API) for each residential area).
  1. Total Number of Dwellings Sold (by type) by Building Area Cluster
    • Analyzing whether the building area cluster which the dwelling units are in affect the number of units sold (both by type of dwelling unit and as a whole).
  1. Effect of Area/Density of building area cluster on the number of dwelling units sold
    • Analyzing the impact and relationship of the Area & Density of the Building Area Cluster on the number of dwelling units sold.
  1. Predicting the Number of Dwelling Units Sold using Density
    • Analyzing the relationship between the number of dwelling units sold (dependent variable) and the density of the building area cluster (independent variable) and to perform prediction on the total number of dwelling units sold

Datasets Evaluation:¶

Only the features of the datasets that are required/used for analysis are listed below.
  1. HDB Property Informationdescribes general statistics on all the HDB Properties in Singapore according to the location of these Residential Units
  • blk_no: Block Number of the Residential Building
  • street: Street Name of the Residential Building
  • year_completed: Year Completed (Start of 99-Year Lease)
  • residential: Residential Property Tag
  • commercial:Commercial Property Tag
  • miscellaneous: Ameneties tag (Childcare, Committees Centre etc)
  • bldg_contract_town: Building Area Cluster
  • total_dwelling_units: Total Dwelling Units Sold
  • 1room_sold: Number of 1-room sold flats
  • 2room_sold: Number of 2-room sold flats
  • 3room_sold: Number of 3-room sold flats
  • 4room_sold: Number of 4-room sold flats
  • 5room_sold: Number of 5-room sold flats
  • exec_sold: Number of Executives sold flats

2. __`Building Area Cluster Names`__ describes the **Names** and **Location** of **Building Area Clusters** according to their **respective prefixes**
  • Legend: Prefix of the Building Area Cluster
  • Name: Name of the Building Area Cluster
  • Latitude Latitude of the Building Area Cluster
  • Longitude: Longitude of the Building Area Cluster

3. __`Planning Areas of Singapore`__ describes the statistics on the **Building Area Clusters**
  • Name: Name of the Building Area Cluster
  • Region: Region of the Building Area Cluster
  • Area:Area (km2) of the Building Area Cluster
  • Population:Population within the Building Area Cluster
  • Density: Density (km2) of the Building Area Cluster

ANALYSIS

When a Residential area is been completed, it denotes the starts of its 99-year lease.

From the graph above, dwelling units with a remaining lease of 64 years tend to be the most popular purchases among Singaporeans.

However, each type of dwelling units have a different trend to how its remaining lease affects it's selling rate. (We will be omitting the analysis of 1-Room Flat as there is too little data for analysis)

For 2-Room Flats, the peak of its sales is with flats with a remaining lease of 97 years. It can thus be said that most of the 2-Room Flats newly developed as there were almost zero sales of 2-Room Flats with remaining leases of 61 to 89 years.

For 3-Room Flats, units with remaining lease of 58 and 64 years have the most sales and there were almost zero sales of 3-Room Flats with remaining leases of 69 to 86 years. This could mean that there may be a low demand for 3-Room Flats during that period of time.

There are constantly a large number of 4-Room Flats being sold regardless of the remaining leases the units. The trend of the sales 4-Room Flats is rather identifical to the trend of the number of residential units completed for each year. it can thus be said that 4-Room Flats are constantly in high demand.

The peak of the sales of both 5-Room and Executive Flats are with flats with a remaining lease of 72 to 79 years. Compared to 3 and 4-Room Flats, 5-Room and Executive Flats became much more popular at a later date.

Based on these analysis, it can be concluded that how the remaining lease affect the sales rate differ rather significantly between the different types of dwelling units.

Insights/Evaluation¶

Based on the analysis that was done above, we have derived certain trends and observations based on the key focus areas that were being identified initally.

Though most of the Residential Areas in Singapore are built to completion in the year 1984, the popularity of the type of dwelling units sold in these residential areas based on the building completion year differ rather significantly from one another.

The number of dwelling units sold are largely affcted by the presence of commercial facilities, and much lesser by the availability of amenities. The most popular type of dwelling units sold in Singapore are 4-Room Flats, with most of the 4-Room Flats sold, being located in Sengkang.

Most of the dwelling units sold are located in the West awnd North-East Region, with Jurong West (West Region) boasting the largest number of dwelling units sold (in total).
